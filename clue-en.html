Why Clue?

We explored the UX case of Clue, the globally acclaimed menstrual cycle tracking app. As the most downloaded and highest-rated app in its category, Clue is a benchmark in user-centered design, transforming the way women manage their reproductive health.

This study stems from our decision to improve Clue's UI in its Spanish version, in line with our mission to advocate for women's health. The analysis not only highlights technical improvements but also emphasizes the app's role in providing accessible, friendly tools that allow women to monitor key aspects of their well-being.

The Challenge
Identify potential improvements in the Clue app through testing, focusing on 3 specific areas: Registration-Onboarding, Start screen, and Tracking.
Develop improvement proposals that deliver a clearer and more satisfying user experience.
Create interactive prototypes evaluated through testing with real users.

Findings
Onboarding
Registration was rated as easy by new users.
They think that the steps to register are sufficient and they have the necessary information to get connected.
They find the design simple and effective.
"Me parece un diseño muy sobrio casi sin identidad"
"The design feels very plain, almost without identity"
CLAUDIA, 36
Start screen
Users appreciate the app's inclusive and neutral language, finding it reliable.
Suggestions include adding cycle alerts for enhanced functionality.
Users prefer consolidating all cycle information onto a single screen for convenience.
Some users find the distinction between the calendar and analytics features unclear.
"Siento que tengo la información pero no la forma de recordarla"
"I feel I have the information but not the way to remember it"
CANDY, 24
Tracking
Users find the tracking confusing as some icons and colors are very similar.
Some buttons interfere with the tracking save flow.
"Me es confuso el uso variado de colores y cómo puedo agregar mis síntomas"
"The varied use of colors and how to add my symptoms is confusing to me"
SARA, 28
Insight
Main Insight

The Clue app is useful, intuitive, and has a simple interface. However, it feels distant because its storytelling and tone prioritize the reproductive aspect of the cycle. This contrasts with the way its users actually use Clue — as a tool for self-awareness and a source of information for their medical appointments.

Design Phase — MoSCoW Prioritization

Following the discovery phase, we prioritize ideas using the MoSCoW tool.

Mo · Must Have
Harmonize fonts, letter sizes, numbers, and icons.
Topicalize and enhance UX writing.
Integrate analysis at the Home screen.
Add reminders to the bottom bar.
Incorporate key icons to enhance the quality of information for users.
Add value to the premium subscription invitation.
S · Should Have
Strategically incorporate the logo and slogan on certain screens.
Modify colors of the 'cycle' graphic on the Home screen.
Adjust calendar color scheme.
Co · Could Have
Integrate an icon into the onboarding for 'What do you want to do?'
W · Won't Have
Implement a discussion forum for the exchange of ideas among users.
