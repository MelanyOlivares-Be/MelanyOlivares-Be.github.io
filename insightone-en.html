Overview — project context
Project Overview
INSIGHT ONE is a complete platform for managing and controlling payment terminals from different brands, covering the entire device lifecycle. It is cloud-based, PCI-compliant, and offers high security, scalability, and real-time access.

The platform uses advanced technology to support omnichannel management of the payment ecosystem, making operations more efficient and secure across different sales channels.
The Challenge
Improve Navigation Clarity and User Flow — users lost track between sections without clear visual feedback.

Establish Consistent Usability Patterns for Information Display — each module had its own visual criteria.

Clearly Communicate the Value Proposition — the platform was powerful but hard to understand from first use.

Research — understand before designing

To gain a better understanding of the product and its users, research was conducted over two sprints of the roadmap. Moderated tests and interviews were conducted with platform users, QA's, developers and stakeholders. Additionally, a desk research and a benchmark analysis were performed. Existing systems were also analyzed through competitive research. This process led to the creation of a persona, the definition of the problem statement, and the development of a user journey map based on insights from the interviews.

User Insights

We identified and organized essential user traits, motivations, and behavioral patterns to better understand how users interact with the product. This process helped us define user segments, discover pain points, and detect opportunities for improvement.

Efficiency and Consistency
A detailed UX analysis shows that users highly value efficiency and consistency in managing payment terminals and remote downloads. This highlights the need for a user-centered approach, focusing on simplicity and standardization to improve the experience.
Simplify Processes and Standardize Interactions
By applying user-centered design principles and leveraging direct user feedback, we can simplify processes and standardize interactions, delivering a more intuitive experience. This approach accelerates task completion and reduces the user's cognitive load.
Guidance
Providing clear and precise guidance — such as character limits for mass messages and defined parameters for bulk data uploads — empowers users to make informed decisions and execute actions confidently.
Segmentation of Features
Effective segmentation of features by user type, along with easy access to help and technical support, reinforces the platform's commitment to user satisfaction and efficient problem resolution.
Task-User Flow

To address this user diversity, we developed a task flow and a user flow to identify and segment pain points specific to each user type within the different sections of the Insight One platform.

Throughout this process, we detected points of frustration and satisfaction, as well as areas of success or failure at touch-points. We analyzed these elements in relation to information architecture, visual hierarchy, and UX writing, which provided us with a comprehensive view of the user experience.

Continuous Development
Users desire a platform with enhanced functionality development capabilities to reduce dependency on providers and meet their specific needs. The standardization of functionalities is a key objective for the client, emphasizing the importance of keeping the platform updated and adaptable to evolving requirements.

User Diversity
The platform is used by three types of users. Clear information about permissions and access associated with different user roles is necessary to avoid confusion and ensure platform security.

Standardized Processes
Users prefer systems that follow standardized processes, highlighting the importance of designing consistent and predictable workflows across the platform. Bulk data upload is crucial for streamlining operation times.
insightone-insight-4.png
Interaction Efficiency
Users value efficiency in interacting with the platform. Therefore, minimizing the number of steps required to complete an action and avoiding time-consuming lengthy forms is essential. Users need detailed information to plan and execute actions effectively.
Defining the MVP — Prioritization and Strategy

After evaluating the ideas with the Insight One team, we defined an MVP using a prioritization matrix followed by the MoSCoW method. The redesign focused on three key areas:

User Paths
Improving User Paths is crucial because a well-designed flow ensures that users can navigate the product intuitively and without friction.
Remove ineffective paths and provide clear guidance
Review icon relevance and ensure visual alignment
Align platform colors with brand identity
Use back buttons or breadcrumbs to mark action paths
User Guide
Enhancing the User Guide is vital because it provides ongoing support, reduces the need for customer assistance, and empowers users with the information needed to make the most of the product.
Standardize language in Spanish, except for specific terms that must remain in English
Review task completion messages to ensure consistency in tone, size, colors, buttons, and screen positioning
Bulk Data Upload
Improving the Bulk Data Upload process is important because it directly impacts user efficiency and system scalability.
Allow manual deletion of terminals with a two-step process
Clearly distinguish between individual processes and bulk data uploads
Provide detailed error messages during bulk data uploads

Obstacles and Learnings
Expectations vs. Capabilities
Companies that start integrating UX often believe that optimal results can be achieved in a single execution. It is essential to maintain continuous alignment between the expectations of the Product Owner and the technical capabilities of the team.
Communication in Beta Phase
Projects in the beta phase provide the opportunity to see the product in action. Open and constant communication with the development team is required due to the platform's ongoing evolution.
Identification of Stakeholders
It is crucial to clearly identify stakeholders, especially regarding sensitive information. Ensuring that the design meets legal and ethical requirements helps mitigate risks and build trust.
Product Scalability
It is fundamental to consider product scalability from the early stages. Establishing a design system that facilitates growth and ensures consistency in future iterations is key.
